02Type

Five display faces. Same sentence, different character.

Body stays Geist. Mono stays JetBrains. This section only swaps the display face — the thing carrying headlines, section titles, and italic-emphasis moments. Each one has a strong personality; pick by which voice sounds most like ZO to you.

Option 1 · current
Instrument Serif
Warm, slightly literary. Heavy use in 2024–25 AI-era landing pages — what you called overused. Soft italic.
A quiet workshop that runs overnight.
Option 2 · variable, has range
Fraunces
Contrast serif with axes for SOFT and WONK. The italic is almost cursive. Editorial + a little playful. Used well by Figma's blog, many indie zines.
A quiet workshop that runs overnight.
Option 3 · editorial, confident
Newsreader
Google's long-form reading serif. Restrained, confident, excellent italic. Reads like a magazine — less trendy than Instrument. This whole page header uses it.
A quiet workshop that runs overnight.
Option 4 · classic, warm
EB Garamond
Old-style renaissance serif. Feels like a research paper you trust. Italic is properly calligraphic. Understated — letting content lead.
A quiet workshop that runs overnight.
Option 5 · modern, tight
Spectral
By Production Type, via Google. Tall x-height, sharp italic. More modern/technical than Newsreader. Good compromise — reads serious, not nostalgic.
A quiet workshop that runs overnight.
Option 6 · no serif · radical
Geist · tight display
Drop serifs entirely. Use Geist at display sizes, with weight + coral as the italic-replacement for emphasis. Reads as Linear / Vercel.
A quiet workshop that runs overnight.
